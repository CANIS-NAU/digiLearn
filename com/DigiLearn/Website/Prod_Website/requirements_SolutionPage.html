The Requirements and Solution

This page will continue to be updated as we work on our project.

  • Requirements: The Digital Backpack requires many developments to become a whole. First and foremost a Linux-based proxy server that integrates with Google Classroom API’s and Google Search. It also manages necessary Oath credentials that are required for a user’s “account/identity” to access Google Classroom services and provides a REST API. Along with that, the Digital Backpack also requires a mobile app that automatically uploads assignments, posts, and queries as well as download new content when the mobile device establishes Internet connectivity. The mobile app should also display content delivered from the DigiPack proxy server when the user is offline.

    This high-level description of the purpose of the Digital Backpack served as the basis through which we continued the requirements-gathering process. From here, we developed a series of user stories that illustrated the needs of various user-types and how the Digital Backpack would satisfy those needs. Those user stories were then further refined into the following domain-level requirements:

    1. The Digital Backpack will aid remote education.
    2. The Digital Backpack will provide seamless transitions between online and offline functionality.
    3. The Digital Backpack will utilize oCDN functionality in order to facilitate asynchronous connections and provide a delay-tolerant network.
    4. The Digital Backpack will support three types of end users: students, teachers, and parents.
    5. The Digital Backpack will be highly accessible in both design and device requirements in order to accommodate a wide range of users.
    6. The Digital Backpack will securely handle all sensitive personal and scholastic user data to prevent unauthorized access.


    These domain-level requirements where then further defined with more granular functional and performance requirements. For a complete overview of the requirements specifications for this project, please refer to our Requirements Specification document.




  • Envisioned Solution: Dr. Vigil-Hayes and our team have set out to create a solution to this problem by creating a network of "opportunistic content delivery" systems, or “oCDN”. As the student comes into range of any public wifi network, the digiPack will opportunistically connect to the internet and begin uploading and downloading essential content automatically. This will allow users with limited internet access to connect to services such as Google Classroom, Khan Academy, Youtube, and potentially limitless other resources. The DigiPack will also submit any assignments or search queries that the user may have generated while they were offline. The end result will be a seamless, networked experience for students without internet access at home.

    To further understand how the architecture of the Digital Backpack system addresses the requirements enumerated above, please consider the following high-level descriptions of our solution vision.

    1. The Digital Backpack aids remote education by retrieving educational resources for the user and providing the user an interface for those resources in the DigiPack app.
    2. The Digital Backpack system provides seamless transitions between online and offline learning by having the user device be completely usable while offline and by having the proxy server work on the behalf of the user while they are offline.
    3. The oCDN functionality between the proxy server and the DigiPack app allows the proxy server to cooperate and do so asynchronously.
    4. The DigiPack app will provide an interface for students and parents. Teachers will be able to interact with the DigiPack app through Google Classroom. So, communications between the teacher and the student will be fascilitated by the proxy server.
    5. The DigiPack app will reach as many users as possible by being available on a large variety of platforms, including Android, iOS and Google Chrome. The DigiPack app will also include multiple, accessible user interfaces that are designed for high usability and targeted towards specific age groups.
    6. The Digital Backpack system will use standard securtity practices to keep user data safe both during transmission over the network and while it is being stored on the end devices. This will be done through encryption. Additionally, the system will authenticate all connections between the devices through OAuth2 tokens.
